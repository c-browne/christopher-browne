Institutional Agenda

A Caribbean development office organized around ideas, convening, and execution.

The site is structured as an intellectual archive and public office rather than a résumé. Its purpose is to present a serious body of work, clarify the development thesis, and direct institutional engagement through The Buckler Group.

From personal profile to public office.

Christopher A. Browne is a Caribbean-American real estate executive, scholar-practitioner, and institution builder focused on the future of Caribbean development.

His work is organized around the premise that the Caribbean must be understood not merely as a tourism market, but as a civilization-scale development platform requiring order, governance, investable institutions, and disciplined public-private execution.

Cornell real estate training informs the Office’s emphasis on entitlement, land-use discipline, hospitality strategy, capital formation, and the institutional conditions required for investable Caribbean development.

Intellectual Centerpiece

The Caribbean Renaissance™ Framework

A regional development thesis focused on institutional order, investment readiness, hospitality-led growth, strategic diplomacy, and disciplined participation by the Caribbean diaspora.

Framework thesis

The Caribbean Renaissance™ Framework positions the region as a unified cultural and investment proposition: many islands, one civilization, requiring better institutions, clearer entitlement systems, stronger convening platforms, and more serious capital formation.

01

Order

Planning, zoning, land-use, approvals, and governance systems that reduce uncertainty for serious development.

02

Hospitality, mixed-use districts, destination strategy, and civic infrastructure as engines of regional competitiveness.
