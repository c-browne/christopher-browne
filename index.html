The Office

A civic and intellectual platform for Caribbean development.

The Office of Christopher A. Browne serves as the personal reference point for a body of work focused on Caribbean real estate, hospitality, entitlement, diaspora capital, public-private coordination, and institutional strategy.

Christopher A. Browne is a Caribbean-American real estate executive, scholar-practitioner, and institution builder working at the intersection of development strategy, regional diplomacy, and long-term capital formation.

His work is organized around a central conviction: the Caribbean must be understood not only as a tourism market, but as a civilization-scale development platform requiring order, governance, investable institutions, and serious public-private execution.

The Office publishes research, strategic papers, and public addresses while supporting executive dialogue on Caribbean development, real estate, hospitality, and institutional strategy.

Grounded in graduate study in real estate at Cornell University, the Office applies an interdisciplinary approach to entitlement, hospitality, institutional development, and long-term capital formation across the Caribbean.
